Occult Obliteration

Occult Obliteration is a 4 player co-op wave survival shoot-em'-up with upgradable weaponry, 4 unique characters with their own abilities, a range of weapons with various stats, and a legion of undead known as "The Possessed" who hunt down the players.

Developed by a team of 5 people in the Unity engine in my first year at UOG, this game implements 3D assets with rigged humanoid characters, particle systems as well as an inventory system, modular weapon and ability frameworks and enemy AI.

Video represents the assignment submission build of the game not the version shown at the COMX event.

My Contributions

My contributions to this project included the overall design of the games mechanics, features and balance; local multiplayer controller support; weapons framework and player inventories, as well as purchasing and swapping weapons; visual effects and lighting, character animation and IK rigging, ability framework for customisable abilities; level management and scene transitions, sound implementation, file structuring and general Unity and C# consultant.

Gameplay

The game spans 3 levels where the players are challenged by 10 waves of enemies, after every round Charon appears in his van and sells players weapons for Soul Points that they can acquire by defeating enemies, the players' weapons carry throughout the 3 levels.

Players are can shoot with their weapons and use their unique abilities to crush the waves of enemies. Throughout the rounds the enemies become more numerous and dangerous, while the weapons the players have access to get increasingly stronger, resulting in chaotic gameplay.

Weapon framework

The weapon framework uses weapon data assets which can be customised and given to a player, by default a player can hold 2 weapons and switch between them, but this could be any amount. Each weapon contains parameters such as its magazine and reserve max sizes, firing type, damage, spread, reload speed, pierce amount and more to be able to create many unique weapons. Aside from the standard weapons, there are also shotguns being the only alternative weapons, which are all based on the "Gun Generic" base class, and all slot onto the controller the same. 

Character Rig

To avoid creating unique animations for all the different weapons which would have taken a considerable amount of time I decided to employ a solution that uses Unity's IK rigs to solve the hands of the character onto points on the weapon. That way I can apply forces on the weapon such as recoil and it will be reflected onto the player character who will move with it. This way I could add more weapon models without worrying about new animations, and since the game had a consistent top down view, it didn't have to look perfect. These IK animations are blended on top of the players other animations.

VFX

I had a lot of fun working on the VFX for this game, from the smoke and embers in the main menu to the projectile and blood effects. I largely used Unity's new VFX Graph features, which offer complex GPU based particles in combination with Shader Graph. All of these come together to add an extra layer of juice to the game.

Abilities

Abilities work similarly to weapons, they can be slotted onto characters and consist of Primary, Secondary and Dash; which effect how a player behaves when performing these actions. Doing it this way meant that all the players used the same scripts, which helped things perform similarly and consistently, and made it much easier to swap out and tweak things as we saw fit. The abilities themselves could be scripted to do anything we wanted but always came from one place.

Player Management

A particularly hard part of part of this game was managing the players between scenes, all 4 players can select their characters in their lobby and they're carried through and paired to the correct devices in the gameplay scene and their items are carried into the next level. This was done through a persistent script that holds references to a players selected character class and gameobject, as well as what device they used to select their character so they are always paired successfully on a scene change.
